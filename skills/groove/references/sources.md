# Groove sources — curated library

Annotated, kept small on purpose. Add a source only after reading it and
writing the one-line "why it earns its place". Distillations live in
`principles.md`; this file is where to go deeper.

## Foundational

- [Roger Linn on swing, groove & the magic of the MPC's timing](https://www.attackmagazine.com/features/interview/roger-linn-swing-groove-magic-mpc-timing/)
  (Attack Magazine interview) — THE swing reference. 50% = straight,
  66% = triplet; 54% = "loosens without sounding like swing"; 50–70% =
  "lots of wonderful little settings". His ranked six keys to groove
  (swing → dynamics → roll feel → timing accuracy → *beat design* →
  interface) — note that no trick saves a bad beat design.
- [Shaping rhythm: timing and sound in five groove-based genres](https://doi.org/10.1017/S0261143023000041)
  (Danielsen et al. 2023, Popular Music — open access) — interviews with
  EDM/hip-hop/jazz/samba producers. EDM verdict: **tight timing, breathing
  dynamics** — most sounds on-grid or ms-scale off; groove is made by
  *choosing and shaping sounds* (attack, duration, pitch: "whmm vs wooohm",
  kick pitch = mood), by dynamics, and by deliberate ms-shuffles of single
  elements or whole sections (track delay). Registers split: low = calm /
  behind, upper = pushing — the samba balanço idea, useful for perc layers.
- [The Effect of Microtiming Deviations on the Perception of Groove in Short Rhythms](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/236893338_The_Effect_of_Microtiming_Deviations_on_the_Perception_of_Groove_in_Short_Rhythms)
  (Frühauf et al.) — the caveat study: *random/systematic* microtiming
  deviations **decreased** groove/liking/naturalness, except shuffle
  patterns. Humanize-by-randomness is a myth; structured swing wins.

## Beat Dissected (Attack Magazine) — genre pattern recipes

- [Deep & Melodic Progressive Techno](https://www.attackmagazine.com/technique/beat-dissected/deep-melodic-progressive-techno/)
  — closest to our melodic-techno lane. 120–123 BPM, ~10% swing; tuned 808
  kit (kick tuned to the track key!); 16th maracas w/ velocity variation as
  the motion layer; two hat layers (one offbeat, one dynamic); sparse
  syncopated conga/tom every 2 bars; rimshot on upbeats *nudged off the
  kick*; clap only at phrase ends, drowned in reverb.
- [Nu-Disco: Live Groove](https://www.attackmagazine.com/technique/beat-dissected/nu-disco-live-groove/)
  — the indie-dance lane. 110–120 BPM, swing 50–60%; kicks on 1+3;
  two claps + snare layered on 2+4 *each offset differently*; 8th hats
  with drawn velocity shapes; ghost snare at bar end; low-mixed shaker;
  pitched bongo/cowbell accents.
- [Rolling Techno](https://www.attackmagazine.com/technique/beat-dissected/rolling-techno/) —
  16th-note low-frequency motion as the energy engine.
- [Beat Dissected index](https://www.attackmagazine.com/technique/beat-dissected/) —
  the whole series; grab a genre when needed.

## Psytrance

- [How to make a driving psytrance beat & bassline](https://howtomakeelectronicmusic.com/how-to-make-driving-psytrance-beat-and-bassline-in-fl-studio/)
  — concrete velocities: closed hats on EVERY 16th cycling
  53/38/78/38%, open hats on the 16ths between (50/78% alternating) —
  the "rushing" texture; claps reinforcing kicks 2+4; sidechain at a LOW
  ratio (1.2:1) for drive without pump; hats panned ~20%.

## Hi-hat / humanization craft

- [Creating Natural Hi-Hat Patterns](https://modeaudio.com/magazine/creating-natural-hi-hat-patterns)
  (ModeAudio) — the four levers: velocity within a narrow band + accents
  that "create and dispel momentum"; tiny manual nudges after tight
  quantize; note-*duration* variation mirroring velocity; **3–5 copies of
  the sample pitched ±15–30 cents, alternated** (fake round-robin — the
  acoustic "different part of the cymbal" effect).
